The Place

There is a version of your team that only shows up away from the desk.

Lake Burley Griffin sits at the centre of Canberra and somehow outside of it. On the water, the hierarchy flattens. Conversations happen that wouldn’t happen in a meeting room — and that, deliberately, is the point.

The Reality

Managers aren’t short on ideas.
They’re short on time.

You have a team that deserves more than another meeting room and a slide pack. What you don’t have is a spare week to organise boats, food, venues and facilitation.

So that becomes our job, not yours. One discovery call, twenty minutes. We confirm your team’s size, what you want the day to do, and a date. From there we arrange everything — the boats, the catering, the venue, the facilitator — and hand you a single confirmed itinerary.

Your only task is to show up with your team.

The Experience

One day. Three movements.

Morning — on the water

The lake does the icebreaking.

Your team boards at the jetty and the work day falls away behind them. Time on the water is unstructured by design — the conversations that surface here are the raw material for the afternoon.

Midday — at the table

Good food. Better conversations.

A properly catered lunch at a lakeside venue — not sandwiches in a breakout room. Shared tables, no assigned seating, and enough time for people to actually talk.

Afternoon — in the room

A session worth the room’s time.

A facilitated working session that builds on the morning — how this specific team communicates, where it holds together under pressure, and what it agrees to do differently. Practical, not performative.

The Session

The afternoon isn’t an afterthought. It’s where the day becomes development.

i.

Psychological safety, in practice

Not the buzzword — the mechanics. What makes people in this team speak up, stay quiet, or check out, and what the team agrees to change.

ii.

Communication under pressure

How the team behaves when deadlines compress. Built from the morning’s observations, not a generic deck.

iii.

Commitments, written down

The session ends with specific, team-authored commitments — captured in the facilitator summary you receive that evening.
